# Brain-Computer Interface

Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) is a frontier field simultaneously unfolding across neuroscience, artificial intelligence, precision manufacturing, clinical medicine, and constitutional legislation. This chapter is not an introductory BCI textbook, but a systematic set of learning notes organized around the axis of **"Intention-to-Action"**.

**This chapter stands alongside [Human-Like Intelligence](https://jeffliulab.github.io/ai-notes/01_AI/03_Frontiers/index.md).** Human-Like Intelligence discusses "how to construct mind on the digital side" (predictive coding, world models, causality, meta-learning); this chapter discusses "how to establish a direct pathway between biological neurons and machines." Together they form two parallel paths toward AGI and embodied intelligence.
